About J.K. Rowling

☛ Joanne Rowling, known as J.K. Rowling, was born on 31 July 1965 in Yate, England.

☛ She is best known for creating the Harry Potter series, which became a global phenomenon.

☛ Rowling conceived the idea for Harry Potter while on a delayed train from Manchester to London in 1990.

☛ The first book, Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, was published in 1997 and received massive success.

☛ The series consists of seven books and has sold over 500 million copies worldwide.

☛ The books were adapted into an eight-part movie series, which became one of the highest-grossing film franchises.

☛ Rowling has also written books under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith, including the Cormoran Strike series.

☛ She has received numerous awards, including the British Book Awards and the Hugo Award for Best Novel.

☛ Apart from writing, Rowling is known for her philanthropic efforts, contributing to multiple charities.

☛ Her rags-to-riches story, from struggling as a single mother to becoming one of the richest authors, is an inspiration to many.
